The Bots

Each bot tags every order with its own client_order_id and only ever sells what it bought itself — three books, politely sharing accounts without stepping on each other.

Stock account · hourly bars

RSI Bot rsibot-*

The original. Watches TSLA, NVDA, PLTR on 1-hour bars and buys fear: when RSI(14) drops below 25 (deeply oversold) it market-buys $5,000, then exits when RSI recovers above 50 — or bails at an 8% stop-loss. The config won a tournament backtest against 10 rival strategies. A challenger (RSI 30/55, no stop) beat it in-sample and out-of-sample once — one more monthly win and it takes the crown under the promotion rule.

RSI(14) <25 buy>50 sell stop −8%$5k/entry 1h bars

Stock account · daily bars

Bot v2.1 botv2-*

The portfolio manager. Scans a ~150-name universe (mega-cap tech core, an AI/semis watchlist, and broad US large-caps) for statistical dips — a close 2σ below its 20-day mean — but only buys names above their 200-day average, and only while SPY itself is in an uptrend. Entries are volatility-sized ($1–3k), take profit back at the mean, stop at −10%, or time-stop after 21 days. It also keeps a 16-name core equal-weighted and benchmarks itself against a "frozen twin" of its own day-one holdings.

dip z < −2trend 200d MA vol-sized $1–3kTP 20d mean stop −10%time-stop 21d

Crypto account · 24/7

Crypto Bot cryptobot-*

Fully segregated: its own $1M paper account, its own keys, its own schedule — crypto never sleeps, so neither does it. Runs trend-filtered RSI mean reversion (buy <30 in an uptrend, sell >55) on BTC/USD and ETH/USD, sizing each entry at 15% of equity. It trades around a permanent ~$500k core — 70/30 BTC/ETH bought as the crypto twin of the stock account's SPY layer — plus a 26-coin alt sleeve, and it can only ever sell coins from its own tagged book. The core is untouchable.

04

The Risk Engine

Autonomous money needs autonomous brakes. Every buy passes through shared, account-wide gates in risk.py before it reaches the broker — sells are never blocked, because the exit door stays open.

🛑

Drawdown kill switch

All buying halts if equity falls more than 5% below its high-water mark — computed statelessly from the broker's own portfolio history, so it can't be gamed by a bot with amnesia.

ARMED · −2.08% vs HWM
💧

Cash reserve floor

No buy may take cash below 10% of equity. The dip-buyer always keeps dry powder for the next dip instead of going all-in on the first one.

ARMED · 15.5% held
📉

Daily loss halt

Bot v2 stops buying for the day if the account drops 2% intraday. Tomorrow is another day; today is done.

ARMED · day −1.19%
📏

Position & order caps

No single name over 5% of equity, max 5 orders per run, no trading in the first or last 5 minutes of the session — no fat fingers, even robotic ones.

STRUCTURAL
🏷️

Book isolation

Every order is tagged. A bot can only sell shares its own tag bought — it can't touch the SPY layer, the crypto core, another bot's book, or my manual trades.

STRUCTURAL
🧪

Promotion rule

Strategies aren't hot-swapped on one good backtest. A challenger must beat the incumbent in-sample and out-of-sample in two consecutive monthly tournaments — and a human flips the switch.

CHALLENGER AT 1 / 2 WINS
05

How a trade happens

From cron tick to phone buzz, with no human in between.

01

A Routine wakes up

The cron fires and a fresh AI agent spins up, checks out main — the single source of truth — and installs dependencies. Feature branches never trade.

02

The bot reads the market

Completed hourly bars only — the still-forming bar is dropped so signals can't peek at the future. RSI, z-scores, and trend filters decide: signal or no signal.

03

Risk gates vote

Cash reserve, drawdown kill switch, position caps, market-window checks. A BLOCKED line in the log is the system working, not failing.

04

Order → broker → verify

Tagged market order to the Alpaca paper API. The agent then re-reads the order status from the broker and reports the actual fill — never the intended one.

05

The human gets one line

A push notification: 🪙 BTC/USD: RSI 27.3 OVERSOLD → bought $150,000. The nightly report and the every-2-hours watchdog keep the receipts.
